Production

Favreau preferred to use practical and instant effects over CGI in the film. "...it's much more fun to actually shoot spaceships or have a real robot running around the set, or real Zorgons built by Stan Winston. It gives the actors, especially the young ones, a lot more work," he said. Dax Shepard, who plays the astronaut in the film, said he wouldn't be interested in participating in the film if the effects were CGI. Actress Kristen Stewart liked the set of effects, saying that, "When we harpooned and wrecked the walls, that really happened.

When there was fire, there was real fire", and that "The only green screen used was when the black hole sucked in the characters". Miniature models were used to create the spaceships, and Favreau wanted to go back to the old techniques used in many previous films, such as the original Star Wars trilogy. However, some Zorgon ships were computer generated, and digital effects were used in many other scenes, such as the creation of meteors and planets, computer generated to add legs and arms because the robot was built by Stan Winston Studios.

David Koepp was born on June 9, 1963 in Wisconsin, USA. He is an author and director, known for his work on Jurassic Park (1993), Echoes from Beyond (1999) and Mission: Impossible (1996). Koepp is the ninth most successful screenwriter of all time at the US box office, with a total earning of $2.3 billion.
